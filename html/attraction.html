Waterville

Waterville Village, also known as "The Little Whirlpool", displays some of the most breathtaking scenery in Kerry. It is situated on the Ring of Kerry and has some of the most spectacular scenery in all of Ireland.

There are many activities to be done in Waterville, sites of interest to visit and beautiful beaches to relax on.

Things To Do

Charlie Chaplin Statue

For the man who made the movies speak in hearts of millions, Charlie spent many years in our midst as a welcome and humble guest and friend to many. The movie career of Charlie Chaplin began in 1913 and he had the longest most versatile career in the history of movies. His movies continue to be enjoyed by youngsters in age and mind to this day.

The sculpture was created by Valentia Sculptor Alan Ryan Hall and was unveiled in July 1998 and has been stopping traffic passing through Waterville ever since.

Golf

Waterville Golf Links which is over 100 years old and is rated among the top 5 courses in Ireland and the top 20 links courses in the world. Recent visitors to the course are Tiger Woods, and Mark O'Meara. The newly completed Skellig Bay Golf course is also a mere 5 minutes away from our B&B. Ron Kirby of the Old Head of Kinsale is the designer of this new course.

Ron has worked with Robert Trent Jones, Gary Player, and later with Jack Nicklaus in Mount Juliet. The opening holes are built on hilly terrain around the meandering Fionnglassa River, and the back nine works towards the cliff overlooking Ballinskelligs below.

Horse Riding

Spend a few hours riding through the local landscape and on sandy beaches or go trekking up the mountains, in the countryside or woodlands. Well equiped Equestrian Centres and Stables are situated in the area specialising in treks to the local beaches and other scenic areas of interest.

Fishing - Lake and Sea Angling

The sea surrounding the Iveragh penninsula is rich fishing ground. The Gulf Stream waters from the Gulf of Mexico heat the offshore waters and provide a variety of marine species.

If you wish to add variety to your fishing, a day of sea angling is highly recommended. Many records for the heaviest types of fish are held in our waters, and Valentia is a noted deep sea fishing location. Fully licensed boats are available, taking parties of Anglers. There are many excellent locations for shore angling (maps available on request). Rods & Reels for hire.

Hill Walking

At over 200km, the Kerry Way is one of Ireland's longest signposted walking trail and also one of the most popular. The trail starts and finishes in Killarney. Looping around the Iveragh Peninsula, the Kerry Way goes anti-clockwise, passing through some of the most isolated and dramatic countryside in the country. For more information visit The Kerry Way

Cycling

The network of safe roads in the vicinity of Waterville are ideal for the cyclist. Many daytrips can be enjoyed such as a trip by the Glenmore Lakes or further a field along the Cumeragh Valley and Lakes and the scenic Skellig Ring.

The Skellig Ring Scenic Drive is without doubt the best section of the world famous Ring of Kerry Scenic Drive and Wild Atlantic Way coastal route. This 'must do' scenic tourist drive is in County Kerry in the south west of Ireland. The 'unmissable' Skellig Ring scenic drive starts in either Cahersiveen or Waterville depending on where you are on the Ring of Kerry and takes in the wonderful Valentia Island accessed by ferry and bridge, the picturesque Portmagee and Ballinskelligs with its great Blue Flag beach, Waterville village and all things these places offer.

Water Sports

There are several water sports centers in the area incorporating tuition and equipment rental together with scuba-diving, canoeing, water skiing, windsurfing and sailing. The area is surrounded by safe and beautiful swimming havens where people can go to escape the pressures of life and enter the adventurous world of watersports.

For more information on Waterville, visit www.visitwaterville.ie
